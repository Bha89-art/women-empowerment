Processing the Trauma

Manage Fear

Try the following exercise to manage fear:

  • Write down what you are afraid of and explore why it scares you.
  • Practice grounding techniques: focus on your surroundings and describe them in detail to distract yourself from fear.
  • Engage in a calming activity, such as listening to soothing music or taking a warm bath.

Manage Shame

Try the following exercise to manage shame:

  • Write down the source of your shame and challenge any negative beliefs about yourself.
  • Talk to someone you trust about your feelings and get a different perspective.
  • Practice self-compassion: treat yourself with the same kindness and understanding that you would offer a friend.

Psychodynamic Psychotherapy

Psychodynamic psychotherapy focuses on exploring the deep-seated, often unconscious, emotions and experiences that influence current behavior. It involves delving into past relationships and experiences to understand and resolve current issues.

  • Helps individuals understand and process unresolved feelings from past experiences.
  • Encourages exploration of how past relationships and experiences shape current behavior and emotions.
  • Aims to improve self-awareness and emotional understanding.

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)

  • EMDR utilizes bilateral stimulation to reprocess traumatic memories.
  • It aims to alleviate distress and promote emotional healing.
  • EMDR is structured psychotherapy for trauma-related disorders.
